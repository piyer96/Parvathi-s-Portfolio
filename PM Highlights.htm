Key Highlights:

1)      Can you walk me through a project you managed from start to finish, including how you ensured it stayed within scope, budget, and schedule?

a.     As an example: Zelle core payment resiliency initiative:

(1)   Developed charter for program as soon as I was roped in as AgilePM to lock down the

program goals, assumptions, risk & issue log, success matrix, stakeholder matrix, high

level technology impact, resources and capacity requirements.

(2)   Confirmed scope, budget and timeline of each of 5 projects for 2024 in charter. Worked with managers to onboard required functional resources, worked with product & business analyst to confirm features backlog, kickedoff the project, facilitated bi-weekly sprint planning, weekly backlog reviews/priortization, monitored issues/risks and rolled on my sleeves to help team with issues and risks (RAID, ROAM logs). Facilitated PI planning, demo sessions, coached product & technology team to prep for PIs. Worked in Scrum Of Scrumwith Scrum Masters or with Project Managers in SAFE format

(3)   Reviewed frequently project updates with stakeholders, PMO, Portfolio. Reviewed detailed project plan daily; statuses with sponsor, leaderships to share updates on project progress (planned vs actuals on timeline, shared resources, scope, cost), any issues that needs or

being provided management support. Kept close eye on scope, budget (Opex/CapEx) &

actuals and timeline, deliverables via detailed plan in MS-Project, PPM tool, JIRA, Confluence, Teams.

(4)   Worked with governance team to review and get signoffs for deployments. Worked with

Operations, gtm team for production readiness (deployment plan, trainings, key steps, timeline) along with change management requirement. Post deployment monitored/signed off to ensured key metrics relating to OKRs are monitored, reviewed and reported to sponsor before getting an agreement to close the project.

 

2)     How do you typically handle competing priorities across multiple projects, especially when supporting different stakeholders like Category Managers?

a.     Multiple projects within one program or as different projects:

(1)   I as PM, develop key detailed plan for different projects, identify stakeholders (stakeholder matrix deliverable), key management and team membersfor each projects to determine communication plans. Establish risks and issues log. Identify critical path in detailed plan of all my multiple projects.

(2)   I daily review project plans around noon time with latest update received in the mornings.

Project teams, stakeholders, management are then shared with how these important steps

from detailed plan are progressing when compared with baseline via email or tools like

teams/slack/confluence. I also try get direction and feedback accordingly on the updates

shared. I ensure stakeholders who can impact the project are provided with upto date project status as agreed with them. For example, network engineers (shared resources) work progress shared with its management so that I can ensure these shared network resources are not pulled out of the project or negotiate to extend their timeline in the project. Same approach to stakeholders who may be impacted by project. These approaches followed through from discovery/intiation till closing of the project.

 

3)      What approach did you take to communicate effectively and ensure transparency?

c.     Project Reporting:

(1)   I as PM, from detailed project plan (baseline vs actuals), provide status updates to the

senior leadership as determined in communication plan. Leadership updates (as decided

in communication plan weekly or bi-weekly or daily as well). Some like it in JIRA or Rally tool whereas some like Excel or Word format via emails or using PPM tool like

Clarity or MS-Project server or Teams, Slack AND I ensure all leadership are provided with updates at same time (all leadership includes Responsible, Accountable, Consulted,

Informed).

(2)   Follow traffic stop light format report and identify actions taken to address anyellow or

red status projects. These statuses include accomplishments, near term milestones, key issues and risks, stop light updates (green, yellow, red) on scope, cost, and schedule AND

any actions taken to address any risks that can impact or issues impacting the scope, cost,

schedule for project to meet the outcomes like change request for additional scope or schedule changes or budget realignments.
